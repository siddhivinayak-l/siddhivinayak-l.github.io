01 — About

I work at the interface of neuromorphic computing, nanofluidics, electrochemical devices, advanced materials, and machine learning. My doctoral research explores how confined ionic systems can exhibit memory, adaptive transport, and memristive behaviour, with the long-term aim of developing energy-efficient computing concepts inspired by biological information processing.

Before joining the University of Manchester, I completed an M.Tech in Energy Systems at Pandit Deendayal Energy University and a B.Tech in Electrical Engineering at Adani Institute of Infrastructure Engineering. My previous research includes machine-learning-assisted analysis of halide perovskites, battery state estimation, electrochemical impedance spectroscopy, and energy-device modelling.

03 — Research

Research
Interests

01
Nanofluidic Memory & Iontronic Systems
Investigating ionic transport, memory effects, and adaptive conduction in confined nanofluidic systems for brain-inspired and energy-efficient computing.
02
Neuromorphic & Memristive Devices
Studying potentiation, depression, retention, endurance, EPSC, IPSC, STDP, and SRDP protocols in ionic and electrochemical memory devices.
03
Electrochemical Impedance & Device Physics
Analysing resistive, capacitive, and interfacial processes in electrochemical systems, halide perovskites, and nanoscale transport devices.
04
AI for Materials & Energy Systems
Developing machine-learning workflows for device-performance prediction, impedance interpretation, battery SOC/SOH/RUL estimation, and structure-property correlation.
