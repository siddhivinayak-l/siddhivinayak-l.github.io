About

Researching how ions can remember, learn and compute.

My work sits at the intersection of physics, electrical engineering, electrochemistry and data science—with a focus on turning fundamental transport phenomena into useful computing functions.

I am a PhD Researcher and Dean’s Doctoral Fellow in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at the University of Manchester. My doctoral research investigates ionic memory, nanofluidic memristors and adaptive transport in highly confined channels.

I am especially interested in the physical origin of memory in iontronic devices: how electrode processes, interface dynamics, nanoscale confinement and ionic redistribution shape non-ideal or multi-state memristive behaviour.

Before Manchester, I worked on machine-learning-assisted analysis of halide perovskites, lithium-ion battery state estimation, electrochemical impedance spectroscopy and intelligent automation. This background helps me connect experiments, models and computation across scales.

Research

Four connected research pillars.

From ion transport to intelligent systems, I combine device experiments, physical interpretation and computational analysis to understand and control memory in electrochemical and nanofluidic devices.

Å

Nanofluidic memory and ion transport

Studying adaptive ionic conduction and history-dependent transport in Ångström- and nanometre-scale channels.

  • Confinement-controlled ionic dynamics
  • Memory windows, relaxation and retention
  • Surface charge and concentration effects
μ

Neuromorphic and memristive devices

Translating biological learning concepts into pulse protocols and measurable states in ion-based devices.

  • Potentiation, depression and endurance
  • EPSC, IPSC, STDP and SRDP protocols
  • Associative learning and adaptive response
Z

Electrochemical device physics

Interpreting resistive, capacitive, interfacial and diffusion processes across complex electrochemical systems.

  • Electrochemical impedance spectroscopy
  • Equivalent-circuit interpretation
  • Electrode and interface artefact analysis
AI

AI for materials and energy

Developing machine-learning workflows for device prediction, structure–property mapping and state estimation.

  • Perovskite performance prediction
  • Battery SOC, SOH and RUL estimation
  • Scientific data analysis and automation
